30-Day Challenges

I like 30-day challenges for the same reason I like county fairs: clear rules, weird ambition, and a definite finish line. Daily check-ins keep us honest.

I also built Challengeboard in an attempt to make this process plug-and-play: view the project write-up or open Challengeboard ↗.

Habit-building

Thirty days is long enough to change the channel in your brain and short enough to avoid existential rebellion. One small daily action starts to feel normal, then obvious.

Accountability

Internal goals are slippery. Daily checkmarks and a finish line are harder to ignore.

Relationship Enhancement

Shared effort creates more follow-through than solo promises, and shared suffering builds intimacy.

Completed challenges

  • Cardio: Hit a minimum cardio benchmark daily (either 10 minutes of vigorous, 30 of moderate intensity, 60 of low intensity, or a mix of these, weighted appropriately)
  • Flashcards: Daily study.
  • Burpees: 63 on Day 1, increasing by 3/day until hitting 150 on Day 30.
  • Gratitude: 3 brief daily gratitude meditations.
  • Ank: Flossing daily. That's it.

Challenge ideas (need co-conspirators)

If one of these sounds fun, reach out and we can do it together.

  • Thank you notes: Write daily thank yous.
  • Reading: Read X pages/day of a physical book.
  • Online class: Daily work for an online class, together.
  • Affirmations: Recite daily affirmations.
